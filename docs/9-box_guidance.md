# Manager Guidance: Using the 9-Box for Talent Assessment

## Purpose
This guide helps managers consistently apply the 9-box framework to assess sustained performance and potential for upward mobility. The 9-box supports development, succession, and promotion discussions. It is **not** a performance review tool or employee ranking system.

---

## What the 9-Box Is (and Is Not)

**The 9-Box Is:**
- A shared language for leadership talent discussions
- A way to separate *past performance* from *future readiness*
- An input to development and succession planning

**The 9-Box Is Not:**
- A replacement for performance reviews
- A compensation tool
- Language to be shared verbatim with employees

---

## Axis 1: Sustained Performance

### Definition
Sustained Performance reflects **consistency over time** using a **rolling 3-year average** of performance ratings from the company’s 5-point scale.

### Performance Scale Context
- **5.0** – Exceptional
- **4.0** – Exceeds expectations
- **3.0** – Meets high expectations
- **< 3.0** – Below expectations; intervention required
- **< 2.0** – Automatic Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Meeting expectations already represents a **high bar** at this company.

---

### Mapping to the 3-Point Performance Axis

- **High Performance (≥ 3.50)**  
  Consistently exceeds high expectations; strong confidence signal

- **Effective Performance (3.00 – 3.49)**  
  Consistently meets high expectations; dependable execution

- **At-Risk Performance (< 3.00)**  
  Pattern of underperformance or inconsistency requiring management attention

> Note: A single strong or weak year does not outweigh long-term trends.

---

## Axis 2: Potential for Upward Mobility

### Definition
Potential measures an employee’s **capacity and readiness** to succeed in roles with greater scope, complexity, or leadership responsibility within a reasonable time horizon (12–36 months).

Potential is about the *future*—not tenure, popularity, or current performance alone.

---

### Signals of Potential
Managers should evaluate observable behaviors across four areas:

1. **Learning Agility** – Learns quickly; applies feedback; adapts to change
2. **Scope & Complexity Handling** – Operates beyond current role; manages ambiguity
3. **Influence** – Impacts others without authority; communicates effectively
4. **Aspiration** – Demonstrates sustained interest in growth and added responsibility

---

### Potential Levels

- **High Potential**  
  Demonstrates next-level behaviors; likely ready within 12–18 months

- **Moderate Potential**  
  Strong in current scope; growth possible with targeted development (18–36 months)

- **Limited Potential (for upward mobility)**  
  Highly valuable contributor best suited for current or lateral scope

> Limited potential for upward mobility does **not** mean limited value.

---

## The 9-Box Grid

| Potential \ Performance | At-Risk | Effective | High |
|------------------------|--------|-----------|------|
| **High Potential** | Misplaced | Emerging | Accelerator |
| **Moderate Potential** | Unstable | Contributor | Anchor |
| **Limited Potential** | Strained | Dependable | Expert |

These labels are **internal shorthand only** and should not be shared with employees.

---

## Interpreting the Boxes

- **Accelerator** – Succession candidate; stretch opportunities recommended
- **Anchor / Expert** – High performers providing stability or deep expertise
- **Emerging / Contributor** – Development focus before promotion readiness
- **Any At-Risk box** – Performance improvement takes priority over advancement

High potential does **not** override sustained underperformance.

---

## Evidence-Based Placement

Managers should support placements with:
- Multi-year performance trends
- Examples of stretch assignments or expanded scope
- Demonstrated behaviors—not personality traits

Avoid recency bias and anecdotal judgments.

---

## Calibration Expectations

Before finalizing placements:
- Compare peers in similar roles across teams
- Pressure-test high-potential designations
- Ask: *Would I confidently place this employee into a materially larger role?*

---

## What Managers Should Communicate

**Appropriate Topics:**
- Development goals
- Skill growth opportunities
- Readiness themes

**Do Not Share:**
- Box placement
- Labels
- Numerical cutoffs or scores

---

## Key Takeaways

- Sustained performance reflects consistency, not isolated outcomes
- Potential reflects future capacity, not historical output
- High value does not always require upward movement
- The 9-box is a decision-support tool, not a judgment

> Use the 9-box to invest wisely in people—not to define their worth.
